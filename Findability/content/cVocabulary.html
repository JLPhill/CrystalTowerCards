Terms

  • Search Engine Crawler – A bot that “crawls” the content of websites to index the data for search engines such as google, bing, yahoo. The indexed data is display onto search engine results for users.
  • SERP – or Search Engine Result Page is the page on which indexed search engine data is displayed for users according to their search entry. The results in order depend on what type of search the user made and what the most relevant websites are in relation to its ranking.
  • URL Slug – Part of a URL structure that indicate the end of the web address after domain. This part of the URL makes your many webpages distinct and unique. This also helps gives some description of the page.
  • Meta Tag – Short lengths of text that summarise a webpage’s content. This appears not on the page itself but on the source code. These help to give information to search engines on what your webpage is/does.
  • Backlink – Links from one website to another website. Also called inbound links.
  • Ranking – The ranking of a website on a search engine result page. Higher is better.
  • Content – Refers to the main content of your website in the form of text, images, videos and other data.
  • Search Intent – The intention of the user’s search on a search engine. They consist of Navigational, Commercial, Transactional and Informational searches.
  • Snippet – Title, URL and a page description usually found on a Search Engine Result Page. Keywords are highlighted.
  • Analytics – The process of analysing and organising data acquired from websites. Data is measured and quantified to get a better understanding of user behaviour to improve business/market/web strategies.
